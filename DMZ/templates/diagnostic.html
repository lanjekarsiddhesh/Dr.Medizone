Why should full body checkup be done?

A full body checkup is a necessity these days. These tests show whether you are fully fit or not. Several diseases are detected at the time of conducting the test. It is done so that they can be treated at the right time. There is no specific reason as to why you must have a test related to full body examination. However, it is very important because if there is any irregularity in the body, it can be treated well in time. Medical tests also reveal the genetic diseases going on in the family from generation to generation.

What happens at Full Body Examination?

As the name suggests, a full body checkup is an examination of a person's full body condition. A diverse range of clinical tests is included in this. With the help of these tests, a person can identify if they have a disease or deficiency. Therefore, they can focus on improving their overall well-being.

What do home healthcare providers generally do?

Home healthcare providers most commonly keep your vitals in check after you have undergone a surgery or injury, promote you to indulge in your favorite activities and household chores, help you in carrying out tasks, etc. They are likely to check your blood pressure, investigate if you have pain, ensure your safety, check that you are taking prescribed medications on time, keep your diet in check, provide you with professional medical assistance such as dialysis and ventilator care, etc. The specific role of a healthcare provider depends on the needs of the seeker. Basically, they can provide medical help ranging from basic day to day support and companionship, to critical life support mechanisms.

For whom is Diagnostic Lab Test beneficial?

Every individual can opt for having a full body examination. A whole-body checkup is not limited to any particular age group or gender but it becomes extremely important for people above the age of 40. This is because people of this age are at the highest risk of numerous diseases. A detailed analysis of a person's body is done through a thorough physical examination. If you have a history of a disease, then, in that case, the need for a full-body test is further increased.

Annual preventive health checkup is very important for three reasons:

  • stress at work:
    • Many of us work in stressful environments where work-related stress accompanies us outside the office too. We remain busy all day on our smartphones and computers. This creates a huge tension in our bodies. Diagnostic Lab Tests can identify the factors that cause an abundance of stress. It can also highlight the effects of said stress. Thereafter, healthcare providers can help with stress management.
  • polluted environment:
    • Pollution is no news. We live in cities where air pollution is a major cause of heart attack or brain stroke. Similarly, the water we utilize has been polluted. As well, our soil is punished with chemicals and antibiotics. Such high levels of pollution impact our way of living and cause serious health effects. For instance, human kidneys and liver suffer the most from our intake of poor food and water. Hormone imbalance is also caused by air pollution. Complete health check-up usually includes liver function test, kidney function test, and thyroid function test. It helps individuals in dealing with the side-effects of all kinds of pollution.
  • Lack of exercise:
    • According to most doctors, a minimum workout of 30-40 minutes per day is necessary to maintain our health. In our busy lifestyles, exercising has become a luxury. Staring at a desk computer all day causes physical discomfort and many troubles. Our sedentary lifestyles lead to a vast pool of health conditions. Knowing these conditions is the first step towards correcting them. Thus, a full- body test can help us uplift our lifestyles.

What is the Full body checkup list?

A full body checkup is majorly inclusive of the following types of tests:

  • Heart-related tests like EKG, ECG
  • ENT checkup and dental consultation
  • Testing of mineral salts in the body
  • Blood test like blood sugar test, LFT, thyroid function test, kidney function, lipid profile test
  • Thyroid test
  • Mammography and pap-smear for women only
  • Prostate cancer, urinary tube screening for men only
  • Urine test
  • x-Ray
  • Pulmonary function test
